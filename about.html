About the Astrological Graph Application

This application allows users to generate an astrological graph based on Vedic astrology principles. It visualizes the relationships between planets and houses, aspects, and calculates a total friendliness score among the planets.

How It Works

Users input the positions of the planets in the twelve houses of the zodiac, along with the Ascendant sign. The application then:

  • Calculates the dynamic house lords based on the Ascendant sign.
  • Determines the natural and temporary relationships between the planets.
  • Calculates a compound relationship and a total friendliness score.
  • Generates a visual graph displaying the planets, houses, and their interconnections.

Understanding the Graph

The graph displays nodes representing planets and houses. Edges (lines) between the nodes represent different types of relationships, each indicated by a specific color:

  • Red Edges: Lordship relationships (Planet → House it lords over)
  • Blue Edges: Aspect relationships (Planet → Planet it aspects)
  • Green Edges: Same house relationships (Planets in the same house)
  • Gray Edges: Occupies relationships (Planet → House it occupies)

Friendliness Score Calculation

The total friendliness score is calculated based on the natural and temporary relationships between planets:

  • Natural Relationships: Based on traditional Vedic astrology friendships and enmities among planets.
  • Temporary Relationships: Determined by the positions of planets in the houses relative to each other.
  • Compound Relationships: Combining natural and temporary relationships to determine the overall relationship between two planets.

The score is adjusted using the formula:

Adjusted Score = ((Total Score + 65) / 116) × 100%

This adjusted score is displayed as a percentage next to the total score.

Special Conditions in Temporary Relationships

Additional conditions are considered in temporary relationships:

  • If one planet is in the 4th house and another in the 10th house, they are temporarily friendly.
  • If one planet is in the 2nd house and another in the 12th house, they are temporarily friendly.
  • If one planet is in the 3rd house and another in the 11th house, they are temporarily friendly.

Using the Application

To use the application:

  1. Select your Ascendant sign from the dropdown menu.
  2. Enter the house positions for each planet (1-12).
  3. Click "Generate Graph" to view the astrological graph and relationships.

Interpreting the Results

The graph and relationship matrix provide insights into the interactions among planets in your chart. The total friendliness score gives an overall indication of planetary harmony.

Technical Details

This application is built using:

  • D3.js: For interactive graph visualization.
  • Netlify Functions: For serverless backend calculations.
  • Graphlib: For graph data structure management.
