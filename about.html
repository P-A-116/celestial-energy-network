About

This application allows users to generate an astrological graph based on Vedic astrology principles. It visualizes the relationships between planets and houses, aspects, and calculates a total friendliness score among the planets. This project is a work in progress, with more features planned for future updates.

How It Works

  • Calculates house lords based on the Ascendant sign.
  • Determines natural and temporary relationships between planets.
  • Calculates a compound relationship and a total friendliness score.

Understanding the Graph

The graph displays nodes representing planets and houses. Edge colors indicate relationship types (Graha Sambandhas):

  • Gray: Occupies relationships (Planet ↔ House)
  • Green: Conjunctions (Planet ↔ Planet in the same house)
  • Red: Lordship relationships (Planet ↔ House it lords)
  • Blue: Sign aspects (Sign → Sign and planets within it)
  • Orange: Planetary aspects with various weights

Temporary Relationships

Temporary relationships are determined by the placement of planets relative to one another within the twelve houses:

  • If two planets are 2, 3, 4, 10, 11, or 12 houses apart, they are considered temporarily friendly.
  • Planets in complementary positions (e.g., 4th and 10th houses) are also friendly.
  • Otherwise, planets are temporarily antagonistic.

Compound Relationships

The compound relationship between two planets combines their natural and temporary relationships:

  • Extreme Friend: +2
  • Friend: +1
  • Neutral: 0
  • Enemy: -1
  • Extreme Enemy: -2

Friendliness Score Calculation

The friendliness score is based on each compound relationship:

  • Extreme Friend: +2
  • Friend: +1
  • Neutral: 0
  • Enemy: -1
  • Extreme Enemy: -2

The minimum score is -65, while the maximum is 51. The score is normalized to a 0-100% range for easy interpretation.

Final Score = ((Total Score + 65) / 116) × 100%

Using the Application

To use the application:

  1. Select your Ascendant sign.
  2. Enter the house positions for each planet.
  3. Click "Generate Graph" to view results.
